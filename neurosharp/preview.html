The Danger of "Just Waiting and Seeing"

For decades, the options were brutal. Pills that might slow decline in some people — with serious side effects and no guarantee. Or the slow acceptance that memory, once lost, was gone for good. Meanwhile, 12% to 18% of Americans over 65 already have mild cognitive impairment. Of those, 10% to 15% progress to full dementia every year. Within five years, up to half will have Alzheimer's or another dementia. Most never get evaluated. They dismiss it. Their families dismiss it. And the window keeps closing.
